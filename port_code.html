Paint Program
Sep 13, 2023


This is a simple paint program that I made. I first started experimenting with lines. My first brush was Waluigi, something really simple that used random colors with slightly random line placement. I liked the sort of 3D effect it was making so I experimented more and created the Star brush. Although I named it Star, when used it resembles a pipe cleaner or a Worm on a String. The Star brush is one of my favorites. The multiple colors give it the slight 3D look I was looking for.

Afterwards, I experimented with images creating Smally, a brush made out of one image of my dog. When that worked I moved to creating Warrior Chess, mixing an image with drawing tools around it to look like streamers. I tried to mimic the shiny streamer look by randomizing the colors and limiting them to a certain range. It worked really well and I wanted to continue to experiment with randomizing shapes and sizes.



Leading me to create Craz Circle and Mystic Triangle. I first created Craz Circle, which is my favorite brush. The bright neon colors were inspired by 80s bowling alley carpet patterns and the randomized opacity is supposed to make it look like bubbles. And when you hold it in place it makes a cool ninja star pattern. The Mystic Triangle brush was harder for me since it was tough trying to randomize the triangle positions and have them keep their shape. It took a lot of trial and error but I finally got it to work. Sometimes the triangles do look like lines but the majority of the time they keep their triangle shape. It is really messy but it is nice to look at. The last two brushes I made, Wooper Whopper and Illuminati, were to combine both concepts. They were mainly just for fun and to see how I could incorporate images more into the brush. They are fun to mess with and I really like how these turned out as well.

Overall, I love all of the brushes I made. They don’t work well to draw an image or drawing with but they are fun to play with and make a cool background with.

Cloudy & Rainy Day
Oct 16, 2023


In this piece I combined two algorithms to make a coding remix. The original code comes from p5.js and they are called Particle System and Sine Wave. I used these to give the impression of weather changing over time.

I first got inspiration from the Particle System. My first thought when I saw it was that it looked like rain. So I went through it to change the color and shape of the ellipses. Turning it blue and randomizing the size of the ellipses to make them look like raindrops.



At first I had static clouds but my professor suggested that I should make the clouds move as well. As suggested, I started experimenting with Sine Wave to see how I can make it look like clouds. I made the Sine Wave into a class and replaced the ellipse with a cloud vector. I also messed with the calcWave and rederWave parts of the class to make it so I can edit them when I call them in the draw function. This allowed me more control of the Sine Waves when I called them in. Making so they weren’t all the same. This was difficult to work on since I didn’t fully understand the code but it was fun to reverse engineer it and find out what I could change.

Pikmin
Sep 25, 2023

This piece is heavily inspired by Pikmin. I wanted to make a small interactive piece with these little guys since they are from one of my favorite games (here are some reference photos on the Nintendo website and here is the link to the comics, they are still updating them!).

This was my first time working with classes but the process was pretty straight forward. However, I did make some mistakes earlier in the process that became bigger problems later on. One of these problems was the placement of the translate function that messed up the x and y placement along with the rotation. Making some of the body parts of the Pikmin unusable at times. That was probably what took the longest to work on and fix.

After that was fixed everything was smooth sailing. I had a lot of fun making all of these little guys and getting them to move across the screen. Using classes instead of vectors definitely made the process of making multiple Pikmin easier. This was a really fun coding project to work on and I am really happy with how it turned out
