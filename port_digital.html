Glitch Art




To make this piece I used Adobe Photoshop and Audacity. I put the images into Photoshop and saved the files as audio so I was able to put them into Audacity. Afterwards, I used multiple audio effects on the audio and then exported them. I then got the image by using Photoshop to convert it back into an image. This was all about experimenting with glitched images and the different effects they can give. It was a very interesting experience which I documented here in more detail. I also have a lot more experiment images here in Google Drive. I had a lot of fun seeing all of the different effects I could achieve from using Audacity in this way.


Photography


In this piece I wanted to bring more awareness to the Missing or Murdered Indigenous Persons movement. I wrote a more in depth artist statement here. To summarize, many cases of missing or murdered Indigenous people have been ignored for decades. So many are fighting back trying to bring awareness to the issue and bring attention to the injustice. Being Indigenous myself I did a performance piece with my mom and documented in photos.

Photoshop

This is a found object piece, where I found a book around my house and used it to make a piece. The book I found was Where the Sidewalk Ends by Shel Silverstein and it brought back a lot of memories. This was my favorite poem as a kid and I wanted to pay homage to it. The boat was made out of pieces of wood nailed together while the roof of it is the book cover. The water is curled book pages and the unicorns are crocheted out of yarn. After I took pictures using a green screen I edited them using Adobe Photoshop.


3D Remix


This is a 3D remix where I grabbed 3D models online and used them to make a new 3D model. All of the 3D models are listed here along with a short story I wrote to go with the model. I did have to break models up into pieces, like the Boo, so I could color and texture the different pieces differently. For the Bo model I had to break the eyes, tongue, teeth, and body apart. But I had fun working on this and figuring out how to work with 3D models
